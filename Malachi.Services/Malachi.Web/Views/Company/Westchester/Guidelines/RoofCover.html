Roof Cover

Built-Up

This option includes built-up roof cover composed of several layers of roofing felt bonded to the roof substrate with asphalt. Built-up roofing is often topped with gravel to prevent degradation from sunlight.

Single Ply Membrane

Single-ply membranes are flexible sheets of compounded synthetic materials that are manufactured in a factory. These are commonly known as rubber sheet roofing products, but are more accurately referred to as EPDM, PVC, or modified bitumen sheets. For steel and concrete buildings, this is the most common type of roof covering.

Concrete or Clay Tiles

This option refers to roof tiles that are made of concrete or clay. These tiles may be attached to the roof deck with mortar, nails, screws, or clips. This option also includes stone and slated roofs.

Wood Shakes

This option refers to wood tiles attached to roof substrate with nails or screws.

Normal Shingle

This option refers to shingles that are manufactured by coating a reinforcing material (felt or fibrous glass mat) with asphalt and have mineral granules on the side exposed to weather. These shingles, which are often referred to as composition shingles, are typically manufactured in strips and are one of the most common roofing materials used in the U.S.

Shingle Rated for High Wind Speeds

This option refers to composition shingles that meet ASTM D-3161 Class F or ASTM D-7168 Class H standards. These are wind-resistance standards that test the ability of shingles to remain attached to a roof at a higher wind speed than standard composition shingles. Products that meet the 1994 South Florida Building Code or the 2001 Florida Building Code have passed these tests.

Concrete Roof

This option refers to flat, concrete roofs most commonly found atop single-family dwellings constructed of masonry or reinforced concrete. Although available for use in all North Atlantic Hurricane Models regions, these roofs are most prevalent in the Caribbean, notably in Puerto Rico and Jamaica.
